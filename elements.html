Elements of Plot

Exposition

This is the beginning of the story. It introduces the characters, setting, and basic situation so readers understand what the story is about.

Rising Action

This is where the story starts to become exciting. Problems or conflicts begin to develop, and events build up toward the most important part of the story.

Climax

This is the most intense or exciting part of the story. It is the turning point where the main problem reaches its peak.

Falling Action

This happens after the climax. The story begins to calm down, and the events start to lead toward the ending.

Resolution

This is the ending of the story. The problem is solved, and everything is wrapped up.
